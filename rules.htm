Range Safety Rules
General Rules - All Sections
  1. All members are required to attend an orientation safety course that may be required by the Association.
  2. All members and guests will sign in and out in the register book provided when entering and leaving the property.
  3. Any damage or problems encountered while on club property shall be noted in register book.
  4. When leaving the building, ensure all lights are off, all windows are closed, heat turned down and doors and gate locked.
  5. No alcohol, drugs or person under the influence of same are permitted on club property.
  6. Members are responsible for their safety and behaviour of their guests. The member must have only 1 guest shooter at the firing line. Guests attending more than three times are expected to apply for membership.
  7. Ranges
    1. Outdoor ranges
      No center fire handguns, rifles, or shotguns are to be fired before noon on Sundays. Members are not to fire handguns, rifles, or shotguns after 9:00 p.m.
    2. Indoor ranges
      No center fire shooting after 9:00 p.m.
      No rim fire shooting after 9:00 p.m.
  8. Annual membership dues are to be paid by September 30th each year. Cash payment will not be accepted. Membership Fees are non-refundable. NSF cheques are subject to a service charge.
  9. Junior members and children are allowed on club property only under the direct supervision of a member.
  10. Members and guests shall ensure the property is left clean after using. This includes picking up empty casings and prperly disposing of used targets.
  11. All members must abide by rules as specified for their section and be familiar with the posted by-laws of the Association.
  12. Members must provide their own targets except on regular club section times and approved matches.
  13. Shooting alone is discouraged for safety reasons.
  14. Handguns and .22 cal rim fire rifles are allowed on indoor range (LEAD bullets only). No center fire rifles are to be discharged in the indoor range.
  15. No steel core, armour piercing, magnum rifle or high powered rifle ammunition is to be used on indoor range, or on steel targets on the outdoor ranges.
  16. All firearms will be unloaded and breeches left open on the firing line when not being used or when others are down range. Loaded firearms MUST BE POINTED down range at all times.
  17. All members and guests must comply with all federal and provincial legislation related to the ownership, storage, transportation and safe use of firearms.
  18. When two or more people are using the range, a range officer will be selected who will beresponsible for the safe conduct and operation of the firing line and range.
  19. The red warning flag must be flown when using property other than the clubhouse (i.e. outdoor ranges and other facilities) and put away afterwards.
  20. It is the intention of the General and Section Rules that the club be used in a safe manner.
    Members noticing safety hazards and/or unsafe shooting practices are asked to notify the shooter and/or a member of the executive.
Hand Gun Section Rules
  1. Only members in good standing and being members of the Handgun section and gaving completed a 12 week probationary period and the Club's safety course may apply for a valid Authorization To Carry (ATT) permit at the Peterborough Fish & Game Association.
  2. An ATT permit to carry allows a club member to transport a restricted firearm for target practice at a shooting range approved by the Solicitor General only.
  3. Restricted firearms must be transported in accordance with Federal and Provincial regulations.
  4. Police officers who join the club are entitled to use their duty firearms without acquiring a permit, but must acquire one for any additional restricted firearms they may wish to use.
  5. There is a 12 week probationary period for all new members to the handgun section. During this period the new member will be supervised by the Club's Training Officer. This requirement may be relaxed at the discretion of the Board of Directors only for experienced shooters transferring in from another range.
  6. New members (approved by the Board of Directors) of the Hand Gun section are required to attend a minimum of 12 visits for safety and guns handling instruction on regular Wednesday business nights. During that time they will attend a safety course conducted by a certified safety instructor in a classroom setting. Police officers are ermitted to brig their duty firearms only at these times during this period.
  7. A restricted firearm is required a Authorization To Transported (ATT) permit to carry.
  8. Members of the Hand Gun section must shoot under supervision at least once each quarter to have their permit renewed.
  9. Members of the handgun section must be at least 18 years old.
  10. The Board of Directors may veto the application for an ATT permit and request additional training if deemed necessary.
Archery Section Rules
  1. Indoor and outdoor targets to be used only with approved archery equipment.
  2. Target Point arrows ONLY are allowed on club targets.
    Broadhead arrows are NOT to be used on club targets.
  3. Crossbows can be used on club targets.
  4. If you wish to use broadhead arrows, you MUST supply your own targets.
Indoor Range Safey Rules
  1. Ammunition restrictions - Hand guns - 22 cal. and centre fire, Rifle 22 cal.. No steel core, armour piercing, magnum rifle or high powered rifle ammunition allowed.
  2. Green light means cease fire, line is safe. Red light means firing line is active.
  3. Ranges are for use by members and invited guests only, except for those shooting events which are open to the public. Members shall be responsible for ensuring that their guests are aware of the range rules, and supervise their actions.
  4. When more than one member is on a range, those present shall appoint a range officer. The range officer shall be responsible for the shooting activities at the firing line and shall give the commands to "commence fire" and "cease fire".
  5. The range officer shall also be responsible for ensuring that all firearms are unloaded during a cease-fire, and that no firearms are handled while any person is down range.
  6. All restricted firearms shall be transported to the range facility unloaded, rendered inoperable by a secure locking device, and contained in a locked container that cannot be readily broken into.
  7. Coming to and from the firing line, firearms are to be unloaded and completely clear of all ammunition. Firearms should not be handled or prepared for firing except at the firing line.
  8. All firearms placed on the firing line bench should have the action open until ready to fire. Any firearms requiring maintenance shall be removed from the firing line to a designated safety area.
  9. Keep the muzzle direction downrange at all times or under your control.
  10. Keep the action or the firearm open at all times except when actually shooting
  11. Shooting only proper targets that have been approved by the club range. At no time shall any member or guest discharge his/her firearm at target stands or frames, metal cans, glass bottles, or any such material that may cause dangerous debris or bullet ricochets.
  12. All metal targets must be reactive (they must move or fall down when hit) and must be positioned at right angles to the direction of fire.
  13. All shooters as well as spectators within close proximity to an active firing line must wear eye and ear protection at all times.
  14. Never allow horseplay, careless handling of firearms, or any other distraction while shooting is in progrss.
    Shooters shall use only target holders erected by the club range, these should be placed so that bullets will pass though the target and strike the backstop below mid-line.
  15. Do not fire at any targets directly on the ground. Bullets striking the ground may be deflected over side berms and backstops.
  16. No person impaired by alcohol or drugs shall be permitted to handle or discharge firearms on the club property.
  17. All members and guests must sign the range log book each time they visit the range.
Outdoor Range Specific Rules
  1. 25, 50 and 100 metre firing lines, only 1 firing line to be used at a time, targets positioned at backstop, firing line adjusted to designated position.
  2. Shooters on ranges 100 metres or longer shall use only target holders erected by the club range. These shall be placed so that bullets will pass through the targets and strike the backstop below mid-line.
