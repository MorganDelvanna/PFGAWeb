FAQ - Frequently Asked Questions
   
Q.Who is in charge of your Club?
A.The Club is run by a voluntary, elected Board of Directors comprising: President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer.
 
Q.How old do I have to be to join your Club?
A.We accept members as young as 12 years of age. Of course, there is no upper age limit. Members between the ages of 12 to 17 years must have parental (or guardian's) consent to join the Club.
 
Q.When is your membership year?
A.September 1st to August 31st.
 
Q.If I join in the middle of a membership year, will I get reduced membership fee rate?
A.Yes. People joining after April 1st will pay a reduced rate. Also, for those belonging to another shooting club and wish to use our facilities during the summer months, PF&GA have a special membership category. Full details can be found on our Membership Info page.
 
Q.What does the membership fee cover?
A.It covers the Safety Course mandatory for each new member, the use of Club facilities and equipment, accident insurance and Club newsletter.
 
Q.What is the process to join your club?
A.It's best if you come out on a Tuesday or Wednesday night to meet the membership and talk to the Section Directors.
 
Q.Do I need to have any shooting experience to join your Club?
A.Not at all. You will be provided with the training necessary for the safe enjoyment of target shooting sports.
 
Q.Can I see your range and ask questions before I decide whether to join your Club?
A.You are welcome to come in to see our facilities at any time that the club is open.
 
Q.My friend is a member of your Club. Can I come and shoot as his/her guest?
A.Yes. This courtesy is granted to all our members. The member may bring the same guest a maximum of 3 times before the guest must join the club in order to continue shooting.
 
Q.What type of equipment can be used by your Club or allowed on your indoor range?
A.Handgun shooters using the indoor range must use lead bullets. The calibre of the handgun must not exceed .45. This is to protect the steel backstock from being damaged. High-powered rifles can't be fired indoors. Archery has full use of the indoor range.
 
Q.What type of equipment can be used by your Club or allowed on your outdoor ranges?
A.Rifles and handguns are allowed on the outdoor ranges. Crossbows and longbows are on the outdoor archery ranges.
